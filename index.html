About

A decade of close work, now building toward something bigger.

My career started at PwC, where I cut my teeth on life science and venture capital audits in San Francisco — the kind of work that trains you to spot risk before anyone else in the room does. From there, I moved into industry roles at some of the most interesting companies in tech: MongoDB during its scaling years, BILL when fintech was rewriting how businesses manage cash, and Bluevine when alternative lending was genuinely new. Each stop added a different angle on how finance actually functions inside a fast-moving company.

"I'm drawn to companies at inflection points — places where the accounting isn't just a compliance function, but a real signal about what's working."

The through-line across all of it has been complex, high-stakes environments: Series E SaaS companies, biotech restructurings, hypergrowth startups where month-end close feels like a contact sport. I've led G&A accruals through corporate restructuring, built out financial reporting infrastructure from scratch, and served as a consultant at Kentik and Vaco when companies needed someone who could step in, get oriented fast, and deliver without hand-holding. I also spent time studying international economics in Barcelona, which gave me an early instinct for thinking across borders.

Now I'm at Pepperdine Graziadio pursuing my MBA in Finance, with a focus on healthcare, media, and technology. The MBA isn't a reset — it's a deliberate expansion. I want to move from executing financial strategy to shaping it, and I'm building the network and toolkit to do exactly that. The next chapter is about bringing a decade of operational credibility into rooms where the decisions are being made, not just reported on.

Accounting isn't a back-office function. Done right, it's the clearest signal a company has about what's actually true — and what's coming next.
